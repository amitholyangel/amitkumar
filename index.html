INTRODUCTION

Soil is a natural resource that covers most of the Earth's land and is made up of minerals, organic matter, air, and water

Composition

Soil is made up of solids (minerals and organic matter), liquid, and gases. Air and water fill the spaces between soil particles, called pore spaces, and make up about half of the soil's volume.

Formation of soil depends upon:

  • Climate
  • Relief
  • Nature
  • sParent rock
  • Time
  • Change in temperature
  • Action of runnning water,wind,glacier etc.
  • Activities of decomposers

CLASSIFICATION OF SOIL

  1. Classified on basis of :
    • Colour
    • Thickness
    • Age
  2. Types:
    • Alluvial soil
    • Black
    • Laterite soil
    • Arid soil
    • Red and yellow soil
    • Mountain soil

Soil profile

A soil profile is a vertical section of soil that shows the arrangement of its layers, called soil horizons. Soil scientists, also known as pedologists, study soil profiles to classify and interpret the soil for various uses.

SOIL TYPES IN INDIA

SOIL TYPES

Type of soil Found in areas Minerals Crops grown Properties
1.Arid soil Western rajasthan and parts of gujrat. Salt Jawar bajra and groundnut. sandy in nature,saline in character. soil lacks humus and moisture.kankars restrict infilteration of water.
2. Mountain lower parts of valleys and mountain slops. limestone and dolomite glass tea and coffee acidic, low humus content,silty in valley slides and coarse in upper slope.
3.Aluvial soil eastern coastal plains in the delta of godavari,krishna,mahandi and kaveri. extends to rajasthan and gujrat. potash, phosphoric acid and lime sugarcane, paddy,wheat, cereal and pulses proportionate share of sand,silt ,and clay
4.Black soil deccan plateau of maharastra CaCO3 and Mg+ cotton and groundnut made up of lava,hold moisture,black in colour
5.Red and yellow soil eastern and southern part of deccan plateau and odisha iron and pottasium groundnut,maize and sorghum reddish colour due to diffusion of iron in crystalline and metamorphic rock.

Important terms

Resources:
All useful elements of environment that satisfy our basic needs are called resources.
Gross cropped area:
Area sown more than once in a year,plus net sown area , is known gross cropped area.
Soil erosion :
The removal of top layer of soil by the forces of nature.
